# Lesson 01 — Rock Foundations While Composing

## Rock in one sentence

Rock = loud backbeat + riff identity + amplified guitar texture + tension/release form.

## Ingredients

### 1. Backbeat

Count 4/4: `1 2 3 4`.

- Kick: often beat 1 and beat 3, plus pickups.
- Snare: beat 2 and beat 4. This is the spine.
- Hi-hat/ride: steady 8ths.

Pattern:

```text
Count:  1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &
Kick:   █ ░ ░ ░ █ ░ ░ ░
Snare:  ░ ░ █ ░ ░ ░ █ ░
Hat:    █ █ █ █ █ █ █ █
```

### 2. Power chords

Power chord = root + fifth, often octave too.

E5 = E + B (+ E). No third. Means neither major nor minor.

Why rock likes it:
- heavy under distortion
- harmonically open
- easy to slide around guitar neck

### 3. Riff

Riff = repeatable musical hook. Usually short: 1 or 2 bars.

Good riff test:
- recognizable after 2 loops
- rhythm has shape
- playable on guitar/bass
- leaves room for vocal

### 4. Blues DNA

Rock often uses minor pentatonic/blues scale over power chords.

E minor pentatonic: E G A B D
E blues: E G A Bb B D

The flat fifth (Bb) gives grind.

### 5. Form

Starter form:

```text
Intro:  riff alone
Verse:  riff + vocal, lower density
Chorus: bigger chords, wider register
Bridge/Solo: contrast
Final chorus/outro: repeat hook
```

## Exercise 01

Compose one 2-bar riff in E.

Constraint:
- Use only E, G, A, Bb, B, D.
- Rhythm must include at least one rest.
- End bar 2 on E or B.

Template:

```text
Count:  1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & | 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &
Riff:   E   E G A   Bb  | B   D B A G E
```
